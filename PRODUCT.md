# StatRoute — Product Context

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
Real-time emergency medical supply routing dashboard. Hospitals submit unstructured alerts; the system uses Dijkstra routing, Gemini LLM parsing, and atomic inventory management to dispatch supply routes and simulate delivery.

## Users
- Emergency coordinators at hospital operations centers
- Hackathon judges evaluating the system live
- Hospital operators managing supply inventory

## Physical Scene
Emergency coordinator dispatching supply routes from a bright hospital operations center on a 24-inch monitor during a high-stakes 3-minute demo presentation. Room is well-lit. Screen must be instantly readable, trustworthy, and visually impressive under pressure.

## Tone
Clinical precision. Trust-inspiring. Minimal noise. Every element earns its place. Data density is high but never cluttered. Actions are clear and immediate.

## Anti-References
- Dark hacker aesthetic (this is medical, not a cyberpunk game)
- Consumer healthcare apps (too soft, too pastel, too rounded)
- Generic SaaS dashboards (boring, forgettable, soul-less)
- Overly corporate EHR systems (too gray, too dense, no personality)

## Strategic Principles
- Information hierarchy over decoration
- State changes must be instantly visible across the room
- Every live event (route dispatch, delivery, circuit trip) must feel significant
- The SVG routing map is the hero — it proves the algorithm works
